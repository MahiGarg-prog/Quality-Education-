Sakal strove to honor his parents' dreams, facing numerous challenges. Often, he studied under dim oil lamps, making the most of every moment amidst the serene countryside. Despite the hurdles, he persisted, seeing each one as a step toward his goals.

Vilas, torn between his parents' struggles, found it difficult to focus on his studies, sometimes due to his own inclination towards laziness. He often found himself looking after his younger brother in his mother's absence or passing time in play and recreational activities.

Sakal, born to hardworking parents in the Chaudhary family, grew up surrounded by their tireless efforts in the fields. Despite their humble life, they dreamed big for him, wishing him to pursue education and opportunities beyond their means. In their modest home, ambition echoed, urging Sakal to aim high.

In the heart of Semapur, Vilas resided with his mother, Geeta, a fisherwoman. Their home, a worn shack by the river, bore the marks of time's passage. Vilas, with his sun-kissed skin and deep eyes, understood hardship early on. His father's absence left them to face poverty's challenges alone, navigating life's turbulent waters together.
